About GreenLeaf

Our Story

GreenLeaf NGO was established in 2016 in Nairobi, Kenya, and is officially registered under the Kenyan NGO Coordination Board *******. From the beginning, we have placed African communities at the heart of forest restoration.

Our governance is locally rooted: the Board of Directors is majority Kenyan, and our executive team is based in Nairobi. We combine scientific methods with traditional knowledge.

Mission, Vision & Values

Mission: Protect, restore and sustainably manage African forest ecosystems in collaboration with local communities.

Vision: Resilient African forest ecosystems, protected by and for local populations.

Values: African local leadership, environmental sustainability, financial transparency, community inclusion, scientific accountability.

Our Programs – Full Overview

GreenLeaf primarily operates in East Africa (Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania) and secondarily in West Africa. All programs are co‑constructed with communities and based on scientific data.

Community Reforestation

Regions: Rift Valley, Mount Kenya, Machakos (Kenya).

Local nurseries managed by communities; exclusive planting of indigenous species; 3‑year post‑planting monitoring. 350 000+ trees planted, survival rate >80%.

Watershed Protection

Riparian reforestation, erosion control, water quality improvement. Partnership with local water authorities. 15 springs protected, 300 hectares restored.

Environmental Education

Green Schools Program: 120 partner schools, 8 000+ pupils sensitized. Teacher training, green clubs, educational outings.

Agroforestry & Livelihoods

Support for smallholder farmers: agroforestry techniques, income diversification, reduction of pressure on forests. 9 400 beneficiary households.
